The word is naturally rare in Plautus, since it belongs to a diction removed
from colloquial language; but there seems no reason for refusing it a place
in his plays. In this passage its ‘raison d'etre⁷ is the appropriation of at
to another use ‘at least’ (v. 684). In the Augu-stan poets it came into
fashion again. They use it as a substitute for at, where the metre requires
a long syllable. In the second cent. A.D. it passed into prose.
